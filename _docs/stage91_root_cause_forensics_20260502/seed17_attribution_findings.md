**Question:** Which sub-layer of `c551b6e` ("cumulative weak-seed fixes") is responsible
for the residual −9.0 seed-17 cost vs the pre-Stage91 baseline (Phase B = 150.50, HEAD =

## TL;DR (Bottom line)

**Movement fix (vector_sim) alone is benign.** Adding the `_apply_movement_behavior`
infrastructure is a no-op on seed 17 (M1 = 150.50, identical to Phase B), because no
behaviors are registered until `vector_bootstrap` is patched as well.

**Blocked-move (passability) fix is strongly POSITIVE on seed 17.** With both the
movement scaffold and the occupied-tile passability check, M2 reaches **174.75**
(+24.25 vs Phase B). Episode 0 jumps from len=145/death=zombie at HEAD to a clean
220-step survival; episode 2 jumps from 162/zombie to 220/alive.

**Two later layers each subtract from that gain on seed 17:**

1. **Activating chase behavior** (vector_bootstrap loads `chase_player`/`flee_player`/
   `random_walk` movement rules from textbook into `model.movement_behaviors`) drops
   M2 → M2b: **174.75 → 152.25** (−22.5). Now that hostiles actually pursue in
   rollout, the model predicts the agent cannot escape contact, and plans collapse
   toward less-aggressive moves.

2. **Feasibility-label first-class scoring** (the `blocked_penalty=−3` /
   `adjacent_penalty=−2` / `displacement_bonus` terms in
   `EmergencySafetyController.select_action`, plus the `effective_displacement_h /
   blocked_h / adjacent_hostile_after_h` fields fed via vector_mpc_agent and
   stage90r_local_policy) drops M2b → M3: **152.25 → 141.50** (−10.75). When every
   move predicts `adjacent_hostile_after_h = True` against a chasing zombie, all
   move candidates score equally bad and the agent reverts to `do` (attack) while
   already at health 2 — terminal trace shows it freezing in place for 6 steps and
   dying.

**Coarse attribution holds:** vector_sim alone = +24.25; everything else in `c551b6e`
combined = −33.25. The dominant negative sub-layer is *movement-behavior activation*,
followed by feasibility-label scoring. The −9 residual is the small leftover after
these three signed contributions cancel.

**Extractor v1 on strong seeds (Phase 2):** mean is exactly **201.42**, identical to
Phase A — but the per-seed picture is +17.75 / −9.5 / −8.25 across seeds 27/37/47.
This is not bit-identity; it is genuine seed-level variance whose mean happens to
land on Phase A. Not meaningful evidence the v1 wiring helps; consistent with the
prior finding that extractor diagnostics are neutral.

This is the smoking gun for the feasibility-label fix on seed 17. The zombie has
been adjacent for ≥ 6 consecutive steps. Every move action would predict
`adjacent_hostile_after_h = True` against a chase-behavior zombie (penalty −2);
the only candidate that does not earn that penalty is `do`/stay. So the
EmergencySafetyController ranks `do` highest, the agent stops moving and gets
hit one tick at a time until it dies — even though *some* move would have been
strictly better than dying in place.

The dominant negative effect of the feasibility-label fix on seed 17 is therefore
not "wrong fix" but "fix is over-penalising any-move-still-adjacent in cases where
the agent is already cornered". `adjacent_hostile_after_h` does not distinguish
"still adjacent and still in chase contact" from "still adjacent but the new tile
is out of attack arc"; both are flagged.

Extractor v1 also flat on weak seeds (existing C-extractor artifact: seed 7 = 155.50,

exactly 0.00, but the strong-seed picture is per-seed-noisy rather than uniformly

Recommendation: do not reopen the extractor v1 direction on the strength of strong-
seed numbers; the visible swing on seed 27 is well within the same noise band as
the seeds-37/47 regressions.

## Bottom line

For seed 17, the residual −9.0 cost in `c551b6e` versus the pre-Stage91 baseline
decomposes as:

```
B → M1   (vector_sim movement-infrastructure)         :   0.00
M1 → M2  (vector_sim blocked-move passability)        : +24.25
M2 → M2b (vector_bootstrap loads chase behaviors)     : −22.50
M2b → M3 (feasibility-label scoring + local-affordances) : −10.75
                                                  net :  −9.00
```

**The offending sub-layer is the vector_bootstrap movement-behavior activation
combined with the feasibility-label first-class scoring.** Either alone (M2b
without feasibility, or feasibility-label scoring on a frozen threat model) does
not produce the catatonic-attack failure mode; together they do. The blocked-move
fix masks the cost in aggregate by adding +24.25 in the same window.

**Next concrete diagnostic step:** in `EmergencySafetyController.select_action`,
make the `adjacent_penalty` conditional on `effective_displacement_h > 0`
(i.e. only penalise moves that ended up still adjacent because the chase caught
them, not stays/attacks that started adjacent). This should remove the freeze-and-
die failure mode on seed 17 ep 0 and ep 2 without giving back the +24.25 from
blocked-move. A one-seed probe on seed 17 with that conditional gate is the
cheapest way to confirm.
