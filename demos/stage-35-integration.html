Key Insight: Cognition Without Backpropagation

СНКС demonstrates that goal decomposition, skill transfer, analogical reasoning, curiosity-driven exploration, meta-learning, multi-agent communication, and long-horizon planning can all emerge from local rules — without a single gradient computation.

How It All Fits Together

СНКС (System of Continuous Cognitive Synthesis) is an AGI architecture that thinks in concepts, not words.


The foundation: FHN oscillators form stable patterns (SKS — Synchronized Knowledge Structures) from sensory input. These patterns are the atoms of thought — bound into compositional structures via HAC (Holographic Associative Codes).


On top of this, 10 capabilities build upon each other:

  1. Goal Decomposition breaks complex goals into achievable subgoals
  2. Transfer Learning moves causal knowledge between environments
  3. Skill Abstraction discovers reusable action sequences
  4. Analogical Reasoning maps skills to new domains via structure
  5. Curiosity drives exploration toward novel states
  6. Few-Shot Learning bootstraps from demonstrations
  7. Pattern Reasoning solves abstract problems via HAC algebra
  8. Meta-Learning selects the right strategy for each situation
  9. Multi-Agent Communication shares concepts (not text) between agents
  10. Hierarchical Planning generates 1000+ step plans with recovery

All of this runs on zero backpropagation. Learning happens through causal observation, count-based novelty, and rule-based adaptation — the way biological systems learn.
