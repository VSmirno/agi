How It Works

Meta-Learning is "learning to learn" — the agent observes its own performance and adapts which learning strategy to use. This is NOT gradient-based MAML; it's rule-based self-observation consistent with СНКС philosophy.

The MetaLearner monitors a TaskProfile:

  • Coverage: what fraction of states has been explored?
  • Skills: how many reusable skills are in the library?
  • Demos: are demonstrations available for bootstrapping?
  • Prediction Error: how well does the causal model predict?

Based on the profile, it selects from 4 strategies:

  • Few-Shot: Bootstrap from demonstrations (Stage 30)
  • Curiosity: Explore novel states (Stage 29)
  • Skill: Exploit known skills + analogies (Stages 27-28)
  • Explore: Balanced backward chaining (Stage 25)

After each episode, the MetaLearner adapts hyperparameters: increasing exploration when stuck, decreasing it when succeeding, and adjusting analogy thresholds based on skill success rates. Result: 1.29x better than the best fixed strategy.
